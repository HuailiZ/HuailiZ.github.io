We present PyroSense, the first system that enables fine-grained 3D posture reconstruction using ubiquitous commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) passive infrared (PIR) sensors. PyroSense detects heat signals generated by the human body and airflow caused by body movement to reconstruct human postures in real time. It advances prior PIR-based sensing designs by improving the sensitivity of COTS PIR sensors to body movement, enhancing spatial resolution without additional deployment overhead, and introducing adaptive algorithms for diverse environments. We build a low-cost PyroSense prototype using off-the-shelf hardware. Experiments show that PyroSense achieves 99.46% classification accuracy across 15 classes and records a mean joint distance error of less than 16 cm for 14 body joints during posture reconstruction in challenging environments.
