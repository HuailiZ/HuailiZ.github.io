Soil monitoring is essential for agricultural systems. Instead of placing sensor antennas above ground, burying them in the soil offers a non-intrusive approach. Low Power Wide Area Network (LPWAN) has demonstrated long-distance, low-power communication for aboveground Internet-of-Things (IoT) applications, suggesting potential for underground cross-soil communication, which remains unexplored. However, varying soil conditions cause significant signal polarization misalignment, reducing reliability. This paper presents Demeter, a low-cost, low-power programmable antenna system that maintains reliable cross-soil communication automatically. We propose a hardware architecture that enables polarization adjustment on commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) single-RF-chain LoRa radios, supported by a programmable circuit for polarization adjustment. We also design an energy-efficient heuristic calibration algorithm and adaptive calibration scheduling method to automatically maintain polarization alignment. Demeter is implemented with a customized PCB circuit and COTS devices and evaluated under diverse soil types and conditions. Results show Demeter achieves up to 11.6 dB SNR gain indoors and 9.94 dB outdoors, extends horizontal communication distance by 4×, supports at least 20 cm deeper underground deployment, and reduces daily energy consumption by up to 82% compared with standard LoRa.
