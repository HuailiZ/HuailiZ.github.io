Accurate leaf wetness detection is essential for understanding plant health and growth conditions. The mmWave radar, with its sensitivity to subtle changes, is well-suited for leaf wetness detection. Existing mmWave-based approaches often use the Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) algorithm to generate image-like inputs and rely on multi-modality fusion with an RGB camera to classify leaf wetness. However, limited understanding of SAR-based mmWave imaging reduces accuracy in diverse environments. This paper presents Proteus, a new method for understanding mmWave SAR imaging. We design a noise reduction algorithm to suppress speckle noise and improve image clarity, and we incorporate phase angle data to enhance SAR texture information, capturing high-resolution surface details for precise wetness assessment in complex plant structures. Additionally, we propose a cross-modality Teacher-Student network, where an RGB-based teacher model guides the mmWave SAR-based student model for feature extraction, transferring explicit knowledge from the RGB to the mmWave domain. We prototype Proteus using commercial mmWave radar. Evaluation shows that Proteus achieves 96.3% accuracy across varied environmental scenarios, outperforming existing methods.
