Vital signs monitoring is essential in healthcare. While commercial electrical-based monitors such as SpO₂-based ECG sensors are available, they are not always suitable for long-term use. Therefore, there is a need for non-invasive and stable systems for long-term monitoring. Fiber optic sensors have attracted significant interest for this purpose due to their small size, high sensitivity, affordability, and immunity to electromagnetic interference. Blood flow in the vascular system produces shifts in the body’s center of mass with each heartbeat, generating micro-movements that can be recorded as ballistocardiography (BCG) signals. This paper proposes a fiber optic sensor based on a Sagnac interferometer (SI) with polarization-maintaining fiber (PMF) for long-term, non-invasive monitoring under a mattress. The system is built on a quartz mesh with vertical mapping architecture.
