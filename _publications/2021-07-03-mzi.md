Vital signs monitoring is important in healthcare. Traditional electrical-based sensors such as SpO₂-based ECG sensors are not always suitable for long-term monitoring due to discomfort. Optical-fiber sensors provide a more comfortable and non-invasive alternative for patient monitoring. Blood flow in the vascular system produces changes in the body’s center of mass with each heartbeat, and the resulting micro-movements are captured as ballistocardiography (BCG) signals. This paper proposes a novel Beat-to-Beat Heart Rate Estimation algorithm using MZI-BCG signals with hierarchical clustering for cardiac monitoring. A heartbeat template is adaptively inferred from the signal using hierarchical and density clustering. Beat-to-beat intervals are then detected through matched-filtering and cross-correlation. The system is based on a Mach-Zehnder Interferometer (MZI) with phase demodulation.
