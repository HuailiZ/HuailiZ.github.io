With the advancement of AI-powered personal voice assistants, speaker authentication via earbuds has become increasingly important as a key interface between users and mobile devices. However, existing audio-based authentication methods fail to defend against voice spoofing threats such as replay and deepfake attacks. To address these risks, we introduce PiezoBud, a practical and lightweight multi-modal authentication system for earbuds. PiezoBud uses miniature piezoelectric sensors to detect micro-vibrations on the skin, extracting user-specific biometric data to authenticate legitimate access on the local smartphone and protect against malicious attacks. Our study with 85 participants demonstrates the effectiveness of PiezoBud in everyday scenarios, including ambient noise, body movement, and in-ear media playback. Using only 15 seconds of enrollment data, PiezoBud achieves an Equal Error Rate (EER) of 1.05% and a mean authentication latency of 0.06 seconds on mobile devices. We also evaluate its robustness against adaptive attacks and its performance in real-world situations. Overall, PiezoBud is practical, resilient, responsive, and secure for earbuds.
